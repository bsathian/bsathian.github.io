About

I am a PhD student working in experimental high energy physics with Prof. Frank Wuerthwein and Prof. Avi Yagil at UC San Diego. I am a part of the CMS Collaboration at the Large Hadron Collider, situated at CERN. My work involves finding new physics beyond the standard model, and developing new tracking software for the next generation of colliders (The High Luminosity LHC). I got my undergraduate degree in physics at the Indian Institute of Science in 2017.

I grew up in sunny tropical Chennai on the eastern coast of India and lived there till I finished high school in 2013. I then moved to the much cooler and pleasant Bangalore (also known as Bengaluru) for undergrad. With my move to San Diego for grad school I'm back to sunny coastal territory.

Aside of work, I enjoy reading and watching cricket.
